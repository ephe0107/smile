      Project impact

      Advancing youth oral health literacy through interactive prevention education

      20 to 32 Smile Check connects preventive dentistry, education, and data-informed outreach in a format that can support children, parents, schools, and dental professionals.

      Mission: To improve oral health literacy, preventive behaviours, and long-term oral health outcomes among children and adolescents through evidence-based, interactive, and accessible education.

      Problem

      Preventable oral disease remains common

      Childhood oral disease remains one of the most common preventable health conditions.

      Goal

      Build oral health literacy early

      Improve youth oral health literacy through interactive education that makes prevention understandable, measurable, and actionable.

      Platform features

      Educational tools built into the platform

      Smile Score Assessment

      Turns daily habits into a clear prevention score.

      Personalized Recommendations

      Explains specific behavior changes based on quiz responses.

      Myth Quiz

      Corrects common misconceptions with instant feedback.

      Progress Tracking

      Shows improvement or decline across completed quizzes.

      Educational Resources

      Includes development guidance, reports, facts, and dashboards.

      University of Sydney DMD curriculum alignment

      Integrated themes demonstrated by the platform

      Clinical Sciences and Skills

      Links plaque, gingivitis, erosion, eruption patterns, orthodontic hygiene, and injury prevention to daily habits.

      Oral and Systemic Biosciences

      Explains biofilm, acid production, saliva, fluoride, inflammation, and oral-systemic health in youth-friendly language.

      Evidence-Based Practice

      Recommendations include why the habit matters and the science behind the advice.

      Prevention and Health Promotion

      Prioritizes self-management, prevention goals, streaks, and habit formation over treatment.

      Social and Cultural Oral Health

      Explores access barriers such as cost, transport, dental anxiety, and regular care without blame.

      Leadership and Communication

      Translates scientific concepts into language children, parents, and communities can use.

      Interprofessional Collaboration

      Shows how dental and health professionals work together to support oral health.

      Why communication matters in dentistry

      Turning science into action

      Children and families are more likely to act on oral health advice when it is clear, practical, and connected to daily life. This platform translates clinical and bioscience concepts into short explanations, visuals, and interactive feedback so users understand not only what to do, but why it matters.

      About this project

      Preventive oral health education for youth

      20 to 32 Smile Check is an educational project that helps young people connect everyday choices with oral health. The goal is to make prevention feel simple, encouraging, and interactive.
